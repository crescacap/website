Our Partners

HT

Founder & Partner

Heloise Taillet

Heloise advises on M&A and fundraising as a Registered Representative with Cresca Capital, a life sciences-focused advisory firm (neuro/CNS, pain, women's health, and longevity), and as a Partner at Luminarc Labs, where she works with investors and startups on high-impact projects.

Her hands-on deal experience spans the full lifecycle — from sourcing and diligence through execution and closing. She has managed the acquisition of a Stanford IP-backed biotech asset, executed the launch of a biotech-focused hedge fund, and advised several biotech startups on strategic projects.

Prior to these roles, she sourced and diligenced acquisition and investment opportunities at Saltwater Capital (family office of Ryan Graves, Uber's first CEO), Thrasio (Series D unicorn startup), and Secocha Ventures (early-stage VC). She began her career as a Summer Analyst at Bank of America and Deutsche Bank.

BA, Economics-Mathematics — Columbia College, Columbia University
Series 79, Series 7, Series 63 — Finalis Securities (FINRA/SIPC)
Former: Saltwater Capital · Thrasio · Secocha Ventures · BofA · Deutsche Bank
VM

Head of IR & Strategy

Dr. Victoria Meissner

Dr. Meissner is a physician–scientist, experienced biotech executive, and investment banker with broad expertise across immunology, oncology, infectious diseases, and metabolic disorders. She brings a rare combination of deep scientific expertise, capital markets knowledge, broad investor network, and a strong track record in corporate strategy and capital raising.

Previously, she served as Vice President Strategy & Global Head of Investor Relations at BioNTech (BNTX), where she led the transformation of the company's external narrative — repositioning it from a COVID-19 vaccine biotech to a global oncology leader. She reshaped the IR function from the ground up, built the earnings and financial forecasting processes, redesigned the Disclosure Protocol alongside Legal, and participated in Business Development transactions including the licensing of a PD-L1/VEGF bispecific antibody and support of the Biotheus acquisition. She joined in early 2023 when BioNTech's market cap was approximately $30 billion.

Prior to BioNTech, Victoria worked at Jefferies advising biotech clients on capital raising (PIPE, Follow-On, Private Placement, IPO) and M&A, and at Westwicke, where she first supported BioNTech through multiple private financing rounds ahead of its IPO. She is a founding member of the Buysiders Club and serves on the Executive Leadership Committee of the American Heart Association's Heart of the Hamptons chapter.
